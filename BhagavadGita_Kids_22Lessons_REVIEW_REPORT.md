- **Where:** Title area — "(VIVERA)" 
- **Should be:** "(VIVEKA)"
- **Impact:** HIGH — This is the Sanskrit term and must be correct. Parents and educators will notice.

### 2. Page 23 (Lesson 22) — "KRISHNA SAYS" header has a typo
- **Where:** Top-right Krishna Says box — OCR reads "RRISHINE TAS?" which indicates the text may be partially obscured or garbled
- **Should verify:** Make sure it clearly reads "KRISHNA SAYS," in the same font/style as all other pages

### 3. Page 23 (Lesson 22) — "WEEKLY DAILY TIMETABLE" — contradictory words
- **Where:** The whiteboard in the main illustration
- **Should be:** Either "WEEKLY TIMETABLE" or "DAILY TIMETABLE" — not both together
- **Suggestion:** Change to "MY DAILY TIMETABLE" — simpler and clearer for kids

### 4. Page 23 (Lesson 22) — "MOTIVATIONS ACHIEVE!" poster in background
- **Where:** Classroom wall poster behind the kid
- **Should be:** "MOTIVATION" or "ACHIEVE YOUR GOALS!" — "MOTIVATIONS ACHIEVE" is grammatically incorrect
- **Suggestion:** Replace with "YOU CAN DO IT!" or "STAY MOTIVATED!" — more natural for a kid's room

### 5. Page 16 (Lesson 15) — Grammar issue in explanation
- **Where:** Explanation text — "Even highest achievers was once a beginner"
- **Should be:** "Even the highest achievers were once beginners" 
- **Impact:** MEDIUM — Grammar error in a kids' educational book looks unprofessional

### 6. Page 1 (Front Cover) — Title reads "BHAGAVAD GITA" but OCR shows possible styling issue

# SECTION E: AGE-APPROPRIATENESS ANALYSIS (2-10 years)

## For Ages 2-4 (Toddlers):
These kids will ONLY look at pictures. They cannot read the text.
- **What works:** The bright illustrations, Baby Krishna character, and colourful comic strips
- **What doesn't:** The explanation text is too long and complex for this age group
- **Recommendation:** This is perfectly fine — parents read TO the child. The pictures carry the message visually.

## For Ages 5-7 (Early Readers):
- **What works:** Short explanation text, relatable comic strips (bicycle, cricket, broken toy)
- **What's slightly hard:** Lessons like Samatvam, Kala Dharma, and Impermanence are abstract
- **Recommendation:** These kids will need a parent to explain. The comic strips help A LOT here.

## For Ages 8-10 (Independent Readers):
- **What works:** EVERYTHING. The Sanskrit terms, modern examples, cricket references, school scenarios
- **What could be added:** A "Try This!" activity box at the bottom (e.g., "This week, say Thank You to 3 people every day") — but this is optional and would require a design change.

## Overall Age Verdict: The book works perfectly for the 3-10 age range. Parents of 2-year-olds will read it aloud. The illustrations carry the message for non-readers.
