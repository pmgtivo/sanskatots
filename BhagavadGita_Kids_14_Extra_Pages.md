## PAGE 15 — PATIENCE (TITIKSHA)

**Krishna Says:**
"Learn to tolerate heat and cold, happiness and sadness — they come and go like seasons."
*(Bhagavad Gita 2.14)*

**Explanation:**
Have you ever waited for your turn to bat in a cricket match? It feels SO long, right? But the best batsmen wait calmly and prepare in their mind before they go in. Good things take time — like a mango tree that takes years to give fruit. Be patient. Your turn will come!

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A kid at a cricket match sitting on the bench, looking upset — "When is MY turn?!"
2. The kid watches the batsman carefully and practices swings in the air
3. His turn comes — he walks to the crease confidently
4. He hits a boundary! Coach says "Your patience paid off!"

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna sitting under a mango tree with a modern kid in cricket gear, both waiting patiently. The tree has tiny green mangoes. Krishna points to the tree and says: "Even this mango waits quietly to become sweet. Can you?"

## PAGE 16 — NEVER GIVE UP (UTSAHA)

**Krishna Says:**
"One should lift oneself up. Never put yourself down. For you alone are your own helper."
*(Bhagavad Gita 6.5)*

**Explanation:**
Remember when you first tried to ride a bicycle? You fell down — once, twice, maybe ten times! But you got back up every time. That's UTSAHA — the energy to keep trying. Even Virat Kohli was once a beginner who missed easy catches. He practiced every single day. Never say "I can't." Say "I can't YET!"

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A girl falls off her bicycle — scrapes her knee — "I'll never learn!"
2. She watches older kids riding smoothly — thinks "They also fell once..."
3. She gets back on, wobbly but trying — Dad runs behind holding the seat
4. She's riding alone with a huge smile — "I DID IT!"

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna on a tiny bicycle with training wheels, riding alongside a modern kid who just learned to balance. Both are on a park path with trees. Krishna says: "Every great thing started with someone who refused to quit!"

---

## PAGE 17 — TEAMWORK (SAHAYOG)

**Krishna Says:**
"By helping each other, you shall reach the highest good."
*(Bhagavad Gita 3.11)*

**Explanation:**
Think about a cricket team. Can the batsman win alone without the bowler? Can the bowler win without the fielders? NO! Everyone has a job, and when they work together, the whole team wins. In your school project, your classroom, your family — everyone's part matters. Even the person who brings the water bottle to the ground is important!

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A kid tries to build a big Lego castle alone — it keeps falling — "I don't need help!"
2. He struggles, pieces scattered everywhere, looking frustrated
3. Three friends join in — one holds the base, one passes pieces, one reads instructions
4. A magnificent castle stands tall — all four kids high-five — "WE did it!"

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna playing cricket with a group of modern kids on a school playground. Krishna is the captain setting the field. One kid bowls, one bats, others field. Krishna says: "A team that plays together, wins together!"

## PAGE 18 — GRATITUDE (KRUTAGNATA)

**Krishna Says:**
"One who enjoys what is given without giving thanks is truly a thief."
*(Bhagavad Gita 3.12)*

**Explanation:**
Who cooks your food? Who drives you to school? Who teaches you to read? Your parents, teachers, bus drivers, farmers — SO many people work hard just for you! Saying "Thank You" is like sunshine — it makes everyone feel warm. A grateful heart is always a happy heart!

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A kid eats breakfast without looking up from his tablet — Mom is cooking tiredly
2. At school, he ignores the janitor who cleaned his classroom
3. That night, he thinks: "What if nobody did these things for me?"
4. Next morning — he hugs Mom saying "Thank you for breakfast, Amma!" — Mom smiles brightly

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna and a modern kid standing on a stage on Teachers' Day, giving flowers to their teacher. The classroom has a banner "THANK YOU TEACHER!" Krishna holds a small diya and says: "A thankful heart glows brighter than any lamp!"

## PAGE 19 — FORGIVENESS (KSHAMA)

**Krishna Says:**
"Forgiveness is the strength of the strong."
*(Bhagavad Gita 16.3)*

**Explanation:**
Has a friend ever accidentally broken your favourite toy? It hurts! You feel like shouting. But holding anger inside is like carrying a heavy school bag all day — it only hurts YOU. When you forgive, it's like putting that bag down. You feel lighter! Forgiving doesn't mean it was okay. It means YOU choose to be free and happy again.

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A friend accidentally spills paint on the kid's drawing — "My drawing! You RUINED it!"
2. The kid sits in the corner, angry, refusing to talk — dark cloud over his head
3. He sees his friend looking sad and sorry — remembers Krishna's words — takes a deep breath
4. He says "It's okay, let's make a NEW painting together!" — both smile, painting side by side

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna in a school art room with two kids. One kid has paint on his shirt, the other looks sorry. Krishna puts his hand on the angry kid's shoulder and says: "Forgiving someone is the bravest thing you can do!"

## PAGE 20 — BE HAPPY WITH WHAT YOU HAVE (SANTOSHA)

**Krishna Says:**
"The person who is happy within, who needs nothing from outside — that person has found true peace."
*(Bhagavad Gita 2.55)*

**Explanation:**
Do you cry in the toy shop because you want EVERYTHING? Your friend has a new tablet, new shoes, new bag — and you want them ALL? The Gita says real happiness is not in THINGS. It's inside you! The richest person is not someone who has the most — it's someone who NEEDS the least. Enjoy what you already have!

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A kid sees his friend's new expensive cricket bat — "I WANT that bat! Mine is rubbish!"
2. He throws his old bat aside, sulking — doesn't play all day
3. He remembers — his old bat was a gift from Grandpa — picks it up, feels its scratches with love
4. He plays with his old bat and scores a brilliant six! — "This bat has magic because Grandpa gave it with love!"

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna sitting on a simple grass mat with a modern kid in a toy shop. The kid is surrounded by hundreds of toys but looking at Krishna. Krishna holds just a flute and says: "I only need my flute, and it gives me more joy than a thousand toys!"

## PAGE 21 — RESPECT NATURE (PRAKRITI DHARMA)

**Krishna Says:**
"I am the taste in water, the light of the sun and the moon, the life in all living beings."
*(Bhagavad Gita 7.8-9)*

**Explanation:**
Krishna says he lives inside every tree, every river, every bird, and every animal! When you water a plant, you're taking care of Krishna. When you throw garbage on the road, you're hurting him. Nature gives us air to breathe, water to drink, and food to eat — for FREE! The least we can do is say "Thank you" by keeping it clean.

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A kid throws a chocolate wrapper on the ground in a park — "Who cares?"
2. He sees a bird trying to eat the plastic, choking — he feels terrible
3. He picks up the wrapper AND five more pieces of trash — puts them in the bin
4. The park looks beautiful — birds are singing — a butterfly lands on his hand — "The park is thanking me!"

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna and a modern kid planting a small tree together in a school garden. Birds, butterflies, and squirrels surround them. Krishna is watering the plant and says: "When you care for nature, you care for me!"

## PAGE 22 — BE HUMBLE (VINAYA)

**Krishna Says:**
"Humility, simplicity, non-violence, and forgiveness — these are the signs of a truly wise person."
*(Bhagavad Gita 13.8)*

**Explanation:**
Imagine Sachin Tendulkar after scoring 100 centuries in cricket. Did he ever say "I'm the greatest!"? No! He always touched his bat to the ground and thanked his team. That's HUMILITY. When you top your class, don't make others feel bad. When you win a race, clap for those who tried their best too. A tall tree that is full of fruits always bends down.

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A kid tops the exam and brags loudly — "I'm the SMARTEST! You all are dumb!"
2. His classmates walk away, no one wants to sit with him at lunch
3. He sees the school captain who also topped — but is helping a struggling student with maths
4. He learns — next exam, he scores high AND helps his friend study — everyone cheers for him

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna at a school Sports Day podium. A kid on the first-place spot reaches down to shake hands with the second and third place kids. Krishna stands beside the podium clapping and says: "The truly great always lift others up!"

## PAGE 23 — ALWAYS TELL THE TRUTH (SATYA)

**Krishna Says:**
"Truthfulness is a divine quality. It leads to freedom."
*(Bhagavad Gita 16.2-3)*

**Explanation:**
Have you ever told a small lie? "I didn't eat the chocolate" — when your face is covered in chocolate! One lie needs another lie to cover it, then another, then another. It's like building a tower with wet sand — it WILL fall! Telling the truth may feel scary for 5 minutes, but a lie makes you scared ALL DAY. Be brave. Speak the truth.

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A kid breaks a vase while playing inside — "Oh no! I'll say the cat did it!"
2. Mom asks — he blames the cat — but feels guilty all evening, can't eat dinner
3. He goes to Mom — "Amma, I'm sorry. I broke it while playing. The cat didn't do it."
4. Mom hugs him — "Thank you for telling the truth. I'm proud of you. We'll fix it together!"

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna in a living room with a kid standing near a broken vase. The kid looks nervous but Krishna puts a hand on his heart and says: "The truth may be difficult for a moment, but a lie is heavy forever!"

## PAGE 24 — FOCUS YOUR MIND (DHYANA)

**Krishna Says:**
"The mind is restless and hard to control, but it can be tamed by practice."
*(Bhagavad Gita 6.35)*

**Explanation:**
Have you ever tried to study but your mind keeps flying — to cartoons, to snacks, to the playground? Your mind is like a monkey jumping from tree to tree! But even MS Dhoni focuses on ONLY the ball when he bats — not the 50,000 people shouting in the stadium. How? Practice! Start small — try to focus on ONE thing for just 5 minutes. Then 10. Then 20. You'll be amazed!

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A kid is studying but thinking about 10 things — cartoons, pizza, playground, phone — head full of bubbles
2. He sits for 1 hour but finishes nothing — "I can't focus!"
3. He tries Krishna's tip — closes eyes, takes 3 deep breaths, looks ONLY at the book
4. 30 minutes later — homework done perfectly! He has time to play AND watch cartoons — "Focus is a superpower!"

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna meditating on a rock with a modern kid sitting cross-legged next to him. The kid has cartoon/food/game thought bubbles floating away from his head. Krishna says: "A focused mind can move mountains. A scattered mind can't even move a page!"

## PAGE 25 — BELIEVE IN YOURSELF (SHRADDHA)

**Krishna Says:**
"A person is made by their belief. Whatever they believe, they become."
*(Bhagavad Gita 17.3)*

**Explanation:**
Every champion was once a beginner who believed they could do it! When PV Sindhu was a young girl, people said badminton is too tough. But she BELIEVED in herself and became an Olympic champion! If you tell yourself "I'm not smart enough" — your brain believes it. But if you say "I CAN learn this!" — your brain believes THAT too. Choose wisely what you tell yourself!

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A kid stands at the start of a school race — inner voice: "I'm too slow. I'll come last."
2. She remembers Krishna's words — changes her inner voice: "I've practised hard. I CAN do this!"
3. The race starts — she runs with all her heart — sweat flying, eyes focused
4. She finishes 2nd place — huge smile — "I didn't come first, but I proved I'm BRAVE!"

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna at a school badminton court with a little girl holding a racket. She looks nervous. Krishna ties a small friendship band on her wrist and says: "Believe in yourself, and the whole universe will believe in you too!"

## PAGE 26 — STAND UP FOR OTHERS (DAYA / KARUNA)

**Krishna Says:**
"He who hates no creature and is friendly and compassionate to all — he is dear to me."
*(Bhagavad Gita 12.13)*

**Explanation:**
What do you do when you see someone being mean to a smaller kid on the playground? Do you walk away? Or do you speak up? Krishna protected the people of Vrindavan when the big storm came. He didn't say "Not my problem." He lifted the mountain! You don't need to lift a mountain — just saying "Stop, that's not nice!" can change someone's whole day.

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. On the playground — a bigger kid pushes a small kid and takes his ball — "This is MY ball now!"
2. Other kids watch and do nothing — the small kid is crying
3. One kid steps forward — "That's not fair! Give it back. We can ALL play together."
4. Everyone plays together — the bully learns sharing — the brave kid gets a high-five from everyone

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna on a school playground, standing next to a kid who just spoke up for a smaller child. Behind them, a group of kids are starting to play together. Krishna says: "A kind voice is mightier than a strong fist!"

## PAGE 27 — DON'T WASTE TIME (KALA DHARMA)

**Krishna Says:**
"I am Time, the great destroyer of the worlds."
*(Bhagavad Gita 11.32)*

**Explanation:**
Time is the most powerful thing in the whole universe — even Krishna says so! And guess what? Time only moves FORWARD. It never comes back! If you spend 2 hours watching cartoons, that's 2 hours you'll never get back. But if you spend 1 hour studying and 1 hour playing, you get BOTH! Smart kids don't waste time — they USE it like a superpower.

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A kid wakes up Saturday morning — "I'll play games for JUST 5 minutes..." (clock shows 8 AM)
2. He's still on the tablet — clock shows 2 PM! — "Where did the day go?!"
3. His friend knocks — "Let's go play cricket!" — "I haven't done homework yet..."  — sits sadly
4. Next Saturday — he makes a timetable: Study 9-11, Play 11-1, Reading 2-3 — "I did EVERYTHING today!"

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna holding a giant clock next to a kid with a daily timetable on a whiteboard. The timetable has colourful blocks: study, play, eat, help parents, sleep. Krishna says: "Time is my gift to you. Use it wisely — there are no refunds!"

## PAGE 28 — LEARN FROM MISTAKES (VIVEKA)

**Krishna Says:**
"There is nothing as pure and powerful as knowledge. One who walks the path of learning finds it naturally, in time."
*(Bhagavad Gita 4.38)*

**Explanation:**
Did you know that EVERY great cricketer watches videos of their mistakes after every match? They study where they got out, what shot went wrong, and how to fix it. That's called LEARNING! A mistake is not a failure — it's a LESSON. If you got a maths problem wrong, don't cry — find out WHY it was wrong. Next time, you'll get it right!

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A kid gets his maths test back — 4 out of 10 — face falls — "I'm SO stupid!"
2. He crumples the paper and throws it in his bag — "I'll never be good at maths!"
3. His teacher says: "Look at each wrong answer. Let's find the mistake together."
4. Next test — 8 out of 10! — "My mistakes were my BEST teachers!"

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna in a school classroom with a kid looking at his test paper together. Krishna has a magnifying glass on the wrong answers (not the right ones). He says: "A mistake is not the end of the road — it's a signboard showing you the right direction!"

# DESIGN INSTRUCTIONS FOR ALL 14 PAGES

## Colour Scheme:
- Same as existing book — pink/magenta borders, soft pastel backgrounds
- Each page can have a subtle colour variation (light yellow, light blue, light green) to feel fresh

## Illustration Style:
- Baby Krishna: Same cute blue-skinned chibi Krishna with peacock feather crown, golden dhoti/jewelry
- Modern Kids: Diverse Indian children (mix of boys and girls, different skin tones, school uniforms / casual wear / sports gear)
- Setting per page should match the lesson (playground, classroom, kitchen, park, living room, cricket ground)

## Typography:
- Page number: Bold in pink circle (top left)
- Title: All caps, bold, pink/magenta
- Sanskrit term: In brackets, slightly smaller
- "Krishna Says" box: Elegant serif font with peacock feather background
- Explanation text: Clean, rounded, easy-to-read sans-serif (same as existing)

## Comic Strip Style:
- 4 panels (2×2 grid), bottom left
- Simple line art with colour fills
- Short dialogue in speech bubbles
- Shows: Problem → Consequence → Realisation → Solution

## Main Illustration:
- Right side, large
- Full colour, bright, detailed
- Baby Krishna ALWAYS in the scene interacting with the modern kid
- Krishna has a speech bubble with the key takeaway line
