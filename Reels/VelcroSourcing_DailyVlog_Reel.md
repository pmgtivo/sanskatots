## SHOT 5 — THE QUALITY VERIFICATION (0:42–0:55)

**Visual:** Close-up of your hands doing a proper quality test on each sample:
- **Test 1:** Pull the velcro apart — listen for the grip sound. Stick it and pull it multiple times.
- **Test 2:** Rub the surface — check softness vs scratch on skin.
- **Test 3:** Place it on a felt fabric swatch — check how firmly it bonds.
- **Test 4:** Hold a sample up close to the camera — show the texture clearly.

**Narration / Voiceover (methodical, confident):**
> "We tested each one. Grip strength, texture, softness — because this is going on a children's book that a baby will touch and play with every single day. It has to be premium."

**Text overlay (while testing):**
> *Testing grip strength* → *Checking texture & softness* → *Felt bonding quality*

**Final beat of this shot:** You set aside the rejected samples and hold up ONE clearly superior strip — you stick it and pull it confidently.  
**Text overlay:**
> *"This one. Premium quality. ✅"*

**Sound effect:** One sharp, satisfying velcro-rip sound.

## FILMING NOTES

- **Best time to film:** Morning light, near a window — natural light flatters product close-ups
- **Lay out all velcro samples before you start** so the flat-lay shot is ready to go in one take
- **Film the quality testing in one continuous take** if possible — authenticity beats perfection
- **The velcro rip sound is critical** — film in a quiet room, or add a clean sound effect in editing
- **Wear something that fits the brand** — earthy tones, kurta, or casual salwar — not overly styled
- **The backstory segment (pen loss reference):** Keep your face authentic — a slight pause before you say "loss taught us" lands emotionally
