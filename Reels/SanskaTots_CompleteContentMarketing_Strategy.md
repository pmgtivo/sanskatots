## HUMOR & ENGAGING CONTENT — THE DETAILED BREAKDOWN

Since you specifically want humor — here are the **exact content formats** that work best for a children's book brand targeting Indian moms.

---

### FORMAT 1 — "Relatable Mom Struggle" Reels *(Highest Share Rate)*

These are gold. Indian moms share these on WhatsApp groups instantly.

**What it is:** A short skit/voiceover showing a relatable parenting struggle — then your product saves the day (or makes it funnier).

**Example Ideas:**
- *"Me giving my kid an iPad vs. Me watching them trace Kannada letters for 20 minutes straight"* — Show both reactions. The iPad reaction = blank face. The tracing reaction = you crying happy tears.
- *"Every Indian parent to their toddler: 'Sit still for 2 minutes!' Also every Indian parent watching their toddler with our velcro book: 'SIT STILL FOR 2 MORE MINUTES!'"*
- *"My mother-in-law judging everything I do.... until I showed her the Ramayana stories book. She's been reading it to my kid for 3 hours now."*
- *"POV: You bought a ₹2,000 toy that lasted 3 days. Also you: bought our ₹299 activity book that's lasted 3 months."*
- *"The face my kid makes when I say 'no more screen time' vs. the face when I give them the velcro book"* — use exaggerated expressions.

**Hook Formula:** [Relatable Struggle] → [Unexpected Twist] → [Product is the hero or the punchline]

---

### FORMAT 2 — "Nobody Talks About This" Content *(Highest Save Rate)*

**What it is:** Presenting surprising/counterintuitive information that moms didn't know. These get saved and shared heavily.

**Example Ideas:**
- *"Nobody told me that tracing letters in your mother tongue before English DOUBLES reading speed. Here's why..."*
- *"Your 3-year-old isn't being naughty. Their brain literally CANNOT sit still. Science says this instead..."*
- *"The ₹99 coloring book vs ₹2,000 tablet for a toddler's brain — here's what pediatric research actually says"*
- *"Why Indian grandparents reading Ramayana to kids creates better emotional regulation — no, seriously"*

**Hook Formula:** "Nobody talks about this — [surprising fact about kids/parenting]"

---

### FORMAT 3 — "Founder Chaos/Comedy" *(Most Authentic Engagement)*

**What it is:** Raw, unscripted-LOOKING comedy about the chaos of building this business. Moms AND startup folks engage with this.

**Example Ideas:**
- *"Startup advice: Build your product. Also me building my product: [show piles of books in your living room, velcro pieces everywhere, printer calling at 7 AM, toddler destroying your design samples]"*
- *"Things they don't tell you about starting a children's book company: 1. Your kids destroy every sample. 2. You will say 'final design' 47 times. 3. The printer will call at 6 AM on a Saturday."*
- *"Day 23 of running a business from home: I've had 3 cups of chai, 0 lunch, 200 WhatsApp messages, and my daughter is using my book samples as stepping stones."*
- *"Boss: sends email at 9 PM. Also me, being my own boss: sends myself emails at 2 AM, then replies angrily."*

**Hook Formula:** "[What startup culture says] vs [What actually happens]"

---

### FORMAT 4 — "Indian Mom Bingo" / List Reels *(Highest Comment Rate)*

**What it is:** A list format reel where you check off things Indian moms do — audience tags themselves or their friends.

**Example Ideas:**
- *"Indian mom starter pack: Googles 'best activity book for 3 year old' at midnight ✅ Buys it. Wraps it. Forgets to give it ✅ Buys another one 6 months later ✅"*
- *"Signs you're a 'conscious parenting' Indian mom: You have 3 activity books the kid hasn't touched ✅ You follow 12 mom accounts ✅ You feel guilty about screen time but also need 10 min of peace ✅"*
- *"How to know our book is right for your kid: Their current hobby is drawing on the walls ✅ They've destroyed 2 tablets ✅ Their teacher said 'needs to work on fine motor skills' ✅"*

**Hook Formula:** "You're [target customer] if... [funny relatable checklist]"

---

### FORMAT 5 — "Honest Comparison" Content *(Most Trust-Building)*

**What it is:** Brutally honest comparisons — doesn't bash competitors, just shows your differentiator in a funny/factual way.

**Example Ideas:**
- *"Generic activity book: 'A for Apple.' Our activity book: 'A for Aam, Ammi, and Ashtavinayak.' Big difference."*
- *"Other kids books: 'Once upon a time in a land far away.' Our books: 'Once upon a time in Ayodhya, Hastinapur, and your nani's kitchen.' 🙏"*
- *"Difference between a ₹50 coloring book and our books — let me show you in 30 seconds"* [actual hands-on comparison]

---

### FORMAT 6 — "Trending Audio + Twist" *(Best Reach Booster)*

**What it is:** Take a trending audio/meme format and insert your brand story into it. This is how you reach people who don't follow you yet.

**Example Ideas:**
- Use "Main character" trend: *"POV: You're the only book in India that actually teaches kids Kannada"*
- Use "It's giving..." trend: *"Ramayana for Kids + Velcro Activity + Mythology — It's giving 'gift every Indian grandparent will actually approve of'"*
- Use before/after trend: *Before: Kid won't sit still for 5 min. After: Kid obsessed with velcro book for 45 min.*
- Use "Running from problems" trend with text about avoiding screen time

**Rule:** When you use a trending audio, move FAST. Trends die in 48–72 hours. Always have 2–3 "pending scripts" ready to shoot when a trend hits.

---

### FORMAT 7 — "Real Customer / Social Proof" *(Most Conversion-Driving)*

**What it is:** Real reactions — your kid, neighbor's kid, niece/nephew using the books. Even without a paid customer's review, your own child's genuine reaction is gold.

**What to shoot:**
- First time your child opens the velcro book — real reaction, don't re-shoot, keep it authentic
- A 3-year-old struggling to trace, then succeeding — the pride on their face = millions of views
- Grandparent reading mythology book to a child — this hits Indian audiences like a freight train emotionally
- Any child saying "Amma, again!" or asking to see the book again

## YOUR SHOOTING SETUP (Step by Step)

### Step 1: Find Your Spot
- Pick ONE spot in your home with the most natural light. Usually near a window in the morning.
- Keep a clean, slightly colorful background. If you have books stacked behind you — even better (it's relevant to your brand).
- Avoid: cluttered backgrounds, harsh overhead lights, yellow-tinted tube lights.

### Step 2: Set Up Your Phone
- Always shoot in **VERTICAL format (9:16 ratio)**. This is mandatory for Reels.
- Go to your phone camera settings → set to **1080p at 30fps** or **4K at 30fps**.
- Place phone on tripod at **eye level** — not below your face, not above. Eye level.
- Clean your camera lens before every shoot (with a cloth). This matters more than you think.

### Step 3: Lighting
- Position your ring light **directly in front of you**, slightly above eye level.
- Turn off all other lights in the room when using the ring light. One light source = better results.
- If shooting product close-ups (books), use natural window light + ring light from the side.

### Step 4: Audio
- Clip the Boya mic on your collar, about 15–20 cm below your chin.
- Plug into your phone's 3.5mm jack (if iPhone, you'll need a lightning adapter).
- **Record a 10-second test first** and listen back with earphones before shooting the full reel.
- Speak closer to the mic than you think you need to.
- Shoot in a quiet room. Switch off fans/ACs during dialogue portions.

### Step 5: What to Actually Say — The 3-Part Script Formula

Every single reel you shoot (even 30 seconds) should follow this:

```
HOOK (0–3 seconds): Say the most interesting/funny/shocking thing first.
MIDDLE (3–25 seconds): Deliver the value, story, or content.  
CTA (last 3-5 seconds): Tell them what to do next.
```

**Never start with:** "Hi guys, so today I wanted to talk about..." — You'll lose 80% of viewers in the first 3 seconds.

**Always start with:** A statement, a question, or something that creates immediate curiosity.

## EDITING YOUR REELS — STEP BY STEP IN CAPCUT

### Installing CapCut
1. Download CapCut (free) from App Store or Play Store.
2. Create a free account with your Google.
3. That's it. You don't need a paid plan to start.

### Basic Editing Workflow (for every reel)

**Step 1 — Import Footage**
- Open CapCut → "New Project" → Select your clips in order.
- Trim each clip: hold the edges of each clip and drag to cut out the beginning/end dead space.

**Step 2 — Add Auto-Captions (This is Critical)**
- Go to "Text" → "Auto Captions" → CapCut will transcribe your words.
- **Why this matters:** 85% of Reels are watched with sound OFF. If you have no captions, you lose most of your audience.
- Edit any errors in the auto-captions.
- **Font to use:** Bold, white text with a black outline. Set font size so captions fill about 60% of the screen width.

**Step 3 — Add Background Music**
- Go to "Audio" → "Sounds" → Browse or search trending music.
- Set your voiceover volume to 100%, reduce background music to 15–25%.
- Always use "Suggested" music in CapCut as these are already cleared for Instagram.

**Step 4 — Transitions (Keep it Simple)**
- Between scene cuts: Use "Cut" (no transition) or simple "Fade" — nothing fancy.
- Avoid: Spin, zoom-in-spin, glitch (these look amateur unless well-executed).

**Step 5 — Color Grade**
- In CapCut, tap on a clip → "Adjust" → Increase Brightness slightly (+5), Contrast (+10), Saturation (+8).
- This makes your video look more "professional" instantly.
- Alternatively, apply a subtle filter: "Lut" → "Moody" or "Natural" at 30% strength.

**Step 6 — End Card / CTA Screen**
- Last 3 seconds: add a screen text with your CTA. Examples:
  - "Follow for Day X of 60 🙏"
  - "Comment 'BOOKS' to get the launch price early"
  - "Link in bio — Amazon goes live June 1!"
- Add your SanskaTots logo on the end card.

**Step 7 — Export**
- Export at **1080p** (not 4K — Instagram compresses 4K anyway and it takes longer).
- Export and save to your camera roll.

## PUBLISHING ON INSTAGRAM — STEP BY STEP

### Before You Post
1. Write your caption in the Notes app first, then copy-paste.
2. Research hashtags in advance (keep a saved list — see hashtag strategy below).
3. Prepare your cover thumbnail (the first frame of your video should be interesting, or you can choose a specific frame).

### How to Post a Reel

1. Open Instagram → Tap **"+"** → Select **"Reel"**.
2. Select the video from your camera roll.
3. On the next screen:
   - **Trim** if needed.
   - **Add Audio** — if you want to change the music.
   - **Add Stickers** → Use **"POLL," "QUESTION," or "QUIZ"** stickers — these dramatically increase engagement.
4. On the Preview screen:
   - **Cover:** Choose an interesting frame that makes people want to click.
   - **Caption:** Paste your pre-written caption.
   - **Hashtags:** Add 3–5 targeted hashtags (not 30 — Instagram has changed, fewer relevant hashtags work better now).
5. Toggle **"Share to Feed"** ON.
6. Toggle **"Share to Facebook"** if you have a Facebook page.
7. Tap **"Share"** — done.

## CONTENT BATCH SHOOTING (How to Shoot a Week's Content in 2 Hours)

You cannot and should not shoot one reel per day on shoot day. This is how you lose your mind in week 2.

### The 2-Hour Weekly Shoot Session

**Session Structure:**
1. **Prep (20 min):** Write 3–4 scripts. Lay out books/props. Set up light + tripod.
2. **Shoot (90 min):** Record 4–5 reels back-to-back in the same outfit/setup.
3. **Log (10 min):** Label each video file clearly (e.g., "Day8_DesignProcess_Raw.mp4").

**Editing:** Edit 2 reels per evening across 2–3 weekday evenings. Each reel edit = 20–30 minutes once you're comfortable.

**Schedule:**
- Sunday = Script + Shoot 4–5 reels
- Monday–Wednesday = Edit 2 reels each
- Thursday = Buffer (reshoot anything, catch up)
- Post daily from your pre-edited stockpile

**Target:** Always have 5–7 finished reels "in the bank" before posting Day 1. Never post the same day you shoot.

## THE SIMPLE FRAMEWORK: ATTENTION → TRUST → BUY

Everything in marketing does one of three things:

```
ATTENTION  →  Make people aware you exist
TRUST      →  Make people believe you're worth their money  
BUY        →  Make them purchase
```

## THE 3 RULES OF CONTENT FOR BEGINNERS

Burn these three rules into memory. If you remember nothing else from this document:

### Rule 1: Fast Hook or Die
You have 1.5 seconds before someone scrolls past. Your first frame + first sentence must make them stop. If your reel starts with "Hi, so today I wanted to..." → you've lost. Always lead with the most interesting thing first.

### Rule 2: Consistency Over Virality
One viral reel with 200K views and then 3 weeks of silence = zero business impact. Sixty days of consistent 500-view reels = 30,000 people who now know who you are. Consistency beats virality for building a brand. Show up every day.

### Rule 3: Talk to One Mom, Not a Crowd
When you write a caption or record a reel — imagine ONE specific person. "Rekha, 31, mother of a 4-year-old in Bangalore, worried about screen time." Speak to her. Not to "all parents." When you speak to one person specifically, everyone who's like her feels personally spoken to.
