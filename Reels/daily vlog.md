**Camera settings:**
```
Video resolution: 4K 30fps (vlog footage)

Format: HEVC/H.265 (saves storage)
Grid: ON (keeps horizon straight)
Audio: Stereo ON
```

**Storage:**
- Minimum 128GB free space before each filming day
- Back up to Google Photos (free, auto-sync on WiFi) every night

## PART 2 — DAILY VLOG STRUCTURE

### The 3-Segment Formula

Every single daily vlog — regardless of what happens that day — follows this structure. It takes **12–18 minutes of raw footage** to produce a 60–90 second reel.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  SEGMENT 1: THE MORNING HOOK (15–20 sec)        │
│  What is today? What are you trying to do?      │
│  Film this within the first 30 minutes of waking│
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  SEGMENT 2: THE DAY (30–40 sec)                 │
│  3–5 moments from the day. Real, unscripted.    │
│  Work + home + chaos + small wins               │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  SEGMENT 3: THE NIGHT CLOSE (10–15 sec)         │
│  One honest reflection. One line. No more.      │
│  Film after kids are in bed                     │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### The Morning Hook — 6 Formats (Rotate Daily)

Never say *"Good morning, today I'm going to…"* — that's a YouTube vlog from 2014. Use one of these instead:

| Format | Opening Line Example |
|--------|---------------------|
| **The Problem** | *"Today I have to ship 23 orders, attend a school event, and finish the Telugu book cover. It's 7 AM. Let's see."* |
| **The Number** | *"47 unread messages. 3 orders pending. 1 school bag not packed. Morning."* |
| **The Honest Confession** | *"I didn't sleep well. The new book proof kept me up. Shipping it today anyway."* |
| **The Question** | *"Is it possible to run a business and be fully present for your kids? Testing that theory today."* |
| **The Specific Detail** | *"The chai is already cold. It's 6:43 AM. Good start."* |
| **The Stakes** | *"Today's the print deadline. If this sample is wrong again, we push back the launch. No pressure."* |

Each of these is filmable in **one take, 15 seconds, no editing needed** — just point the phone at your face and speak.

## PART 3 — WHAT TO FILM EVERY DAY

### The 10 Recurring Shot Types

These 10 shots cover every possible day. Pick 4–6 each day. You don't need all 10.

| Shot | What It Is | Time Required |
|------|-----------|--------------|
| **1. The Wake-Up Window** | Natural light coming through curtains, chai being made, morning quiet | 30 sec to film |
| **2. The Chaos Minute** | School bag search, breakfast rush, someone crying — real unfiltered morning | Film continuously for 60 sec, use 10 |
| **3. The Work Screen** | Your laptop/phone showing actual work — orders, design, spreadsheet | 20 sec |
| **4. The Hands Shot** | Your hands doing something — packing, writing, typing, chopping vegetables | 30 sec |
| **5. The Kid Moment** | One genuine moment with your child. NOT posed. Wait for it. | Film 2–3 min, use 5 sec |
| **6. The Problem Face** | You reading something frustrating, exhaling, thinking | 15 sec |
| **7. The Small Win** | Order notification, positive DM, child finishing a page — reaction shot | 20 sec |
| **8. The Work-Life Collision** | Business call while cooking. Packing orders while supervising homework. | Film 60 sec, use 15 |
| **9. The Night Desk** | Laptop open late, cup of chai, the quiet hour | 20 sec |
| **10. The Close** | Looking at camera, one honest sentence, fade to dark | 20 sec |

### The "Invisible Camera" Rule

The best vlog footage happens when **you forget the camera is there.** Set your phone on the Joby tripod pointed at the kitchen or desk, hit record, and walk away. Review the footage later. You will find 3–5 seconds of completely natural behavior that no directed shot could replicate.

Do this once a day. Leave the camera running for 5–10 minutes. Pick the best 5 seconds.

## PART 4 — VOICE-OVER SYSTEM

### The 3-Sentence Night VO

After kids are in bed, sit quietly, record this into your phone voice memo:

```
Sentence 1: What actually happened today (factual)
Sentence 2: How it made you feel (honest)
Sentence 3: What it means / what you're taking from it (reflection)
```

**Example:**
> *"The print sample came back wrong for the third time today. I sat in the car for 10 minutes before going back inside. But I know exactly what needs to change now — and that's different from last week."*

This becomes your vlog's closing VO. 3 sentences. 20 seconds. Recorded in the dark on a voice memo app. **Raw audio texture is the point** — don't re-record to sound polished.

### When NOT to Voice-Over

Some days, the footage speaks for itself. If you filmed a genuine kid moment, a real reaction shot, or the work-life collision beautifully — **let it play silently with text.** Over-narrating kills authentic footage.

## PART 5 — THE 30-MINUTE DAILY EDIT

### Workflow (CapCut — Recommended)

```
Step 1: Import all footage from the day (5 min)
Step 2: Watch everything once, mark keepers (5 min)
Step 3: Cut keepers into timeline in 3-segment order (8 min)
Step 4: Add VO audio layer (2 min)
Step 5: Add music (2 min)
Step 6: Add 3–5 text overlays (5 min)
Step 7: Color grade (1 min)
Step 8: Export (2 min)
─────────────────────────────
Total: 30 minutes
```

### Editing Rules — Non-Negotiable

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| **No shot longer than 4 seconds** | Vlog pacing requires momentum |
| **Morning footage = cooler grade** | Creates visual time of day without text |
| **Night footage = warmer grade** | Consistency builds cinematic identity |
| **Last shot always fades to black** | Signals "day is complete" without narrating it |
| **Never use more than 2 font styles** | Amateur vlogs die by typography chaos |
| **Subtitles on every spoken word** | 85% watched on mute — always |

### CapCut Template Setup (Save Once, Reuse Daily)

Create a **saved template** in CapCut with:
- Your brand font pre-loaded (Poppins Bold)
- Your color grade preset saved (warm for night, neutral for day)
- Intro card pre-built: *"Day __ | [Month] [Date]"*
- End card pre-built: SanskaTots logo + Instagram handle
- Preferred aspect ratio locked: 9:16

Opening this template each day saves 8–10 minutes of setup.

## PART 6 — TEXT OVERLAY SYSTEM

Use these **5 text overlay types** across every vlog. Mix and match.

### Type 1 — The Time Stamp
```
[ 6:47 AM ]
```
Small, top-left corner, white text, semi-transparent background. Appears at start of morning segment.

### Type 2 — The Internal Caption
```
"third time this week. still not quitting."
```
Lowercase, italic, centered. Appears over your face during a difficult moment. This is your inner voice made visible.

### Type 3 — The Hard Fact
```
ORDERS TODAY: 14
HOURS SLEPT: 5
BOOKS PACKED: 14
```
All caps, left-aligned like a stat block. Appears over the work desk footage. Instantly shareable.

### Type 4 — The Quiet Win
```
she traced ಕ on her own today. 🤍
```
Lowercase, warm amber color, centered. Over any child/product moment. Says more than a paragraph.

### Type 5 — The Closer
```
"Day 47. Still building.
Still scared. Still going."
```
Appears over the final fade-to-black. White text, slow fade in, remains on screen 2 seconds after video ends.

## PART 7 — AUDIO SYSTEM

### The 3-Layer Audio Stack

| Layer | What It Is | Volume |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| **Layer 1: Ambient** | Real sound from filming location — chai pouring, keyboard clicks, child laughing | As captured — do not remove |
| **Layer 2: Music** | Soft background track | 12–18% |
| **Layer 3: VO** | Your recorded voice-over | 100% — dominant |

**Never eliminate ambient sound completely.** The sound of a real home — the pressure cooker, the school bag zipper, the notification ping — makes the viewer feel present in your life. That presence is the entire value of a daily vlog.

### Music Library — Vlog-Specific

Maintain a **saved audio playlist** in Instagram and CapCut:

| Mood | Track Type | Where to Find |
|------|-----------|--------------|
| Morning energy | Soft acoustic guitar, upbeat | CapCut free library → "morning vlog" |
| Work focus | Lo-fi beats with moderate tempo | Instagram audio → "study lo-fi" |
| Emotional moment | Solo piano, no percussion | CapCut → "emotional piano" |
| Small win | Brief orchestral rise (3–5 sec) | CapCut → "success moment" |
| Night close | Ambient, minimal, fading | Instagram audio → "night time calm" |

**Save 15–20 tracks** across these moods. Rotate them. Never use the same track two days in a row — it makes days feel identical.

## PART 11 — WHAT TO DO WHEN NOTHING HAPPENS

Some days are genuinely boring. No drama, no win, no interesting moment. These days feel impossible to vlog. They are actually **the most important days to post.**

Use one of these 5 "nothing happened" formats:

### Format 1 — The Ordinary in Detail
Film extreme close-ups of mundane things: the grain of your desk, steam rising from chai, your hands on a keyboard. VO: *"Today was just a day. And I'm learning that most days are just days. That's not failure. That's the business."*

### Format 2 — The Running Tally
Text-only reel. No footage needed.
```
Week 7, Day 3.
Books packed this week: 34
Hours of sleep (avg): 5.5
Times I wanted to quit: 1
Times I actually quit: 0
```

### Format 3 — The Process Shot
Film one task slowly and beautifully — packing a single order, opening a print sample, writing one line in a notebook. VO: *"Not every day has a story. Some days are just the work."*

### Format 4 — The Question Day
Post a question to your audience instead of a vlog.
*"Day 34 was quiet. So I want to ask you — what made YOU buy your child's first activity book? Drop it below. I read every one."*

### Format 5 — The Callback
Reference a previous day. *"Day 12, I said the print proof was wrong. Day 34 update: it's right. We ship Thursday. Sometimes boring days are just days where things quietly get fixed."*

# Vlog-Style Reels That Build Product Curiosity
## SanskaTots | Not Daily — But Deliberate

---

## THE CORE PROBLEM WITH PRODUCT CONTENT

Most brands show their product. Curious people buy products they **haven't fully seen yet.**

The goal isn't to showcase your books — it's to make people feel like they're **missing something** until they own one. Every reel should leave a small, unresolved question in the viewer's mind.

> *Curiosity = Information Gap + Emotional Stakes*

You control both.

---

## THE RIGHT FREQUENCY

Not daily. Not random. **3–4 reels per week, on a predictable rhythm.**

| Day | Content Type | Purpose |
|-----|-------------|---------|
| Tuesday | Vlog/Behind-the-scenes episode | Curiosity about process |
| Thursday | Education or cultural reel | Trust + product relevance |
| Saturday | Funny or community reel | Shareability + reach |
| (Optional) Sunday | Tease or story reel | Weekend emotional engagement |

Predictability matters more than frequency. If people know your Tuesday reel always shows something they haven't seen before — **they come back on Tuesday.**

## THE 5 CURIOSITY FORMATS (For Your Brand)

These are not content ideas. They are **structural templates** designed specifically to leave open loops.

---

### FORMAT 1 — "The Unfinished Book" Series

**The mechanism:** You're working on a new book. You show the process in fragments — never the full thing — across 4–6 reels over 2–3 weeks. Each reel ends before the reveal.

**How it works:**

| Episode | What You Show | What You DON'T Show | Open Loop Left |
|---------|--------------|---------------------|----------------|
| Ep 1 | Blank notebook, sketching something | What the book is | *"What is she making?"* |
| Ep 2 | Color palette being chosen, rejected | The actual pages | *"What are these colors for?"* |
| Ep 3 | Your face reading a print sample — expression: not satisfied | The sample itself | *"What's wrong with it?"* |
| Ep 4 | Packing 3 copies carefully, writing something on a note | Where they're going | *"Who is getting these?"* |
| Ep 5 | A child's hand opening a package (face not shown) | Child's reaction | *"What happens when they open it?"* |
| Ep 6 | The full reveal — book, child, reaction, your face | Nothing — full payoff | Purchase intent peaks here |

**Why it works:** Netflix built an entire business on this. Each episode answers one question and creates two more. By Episode 6, the audience is *invested* — they've spent 3 weeks caring about this book before seeing it.

---

### FORMAT 2 — "Something Is Coming" Tease Reel

**The mechanism:** 20–25 second reel. Blurred, shadowed, or partially obscured footage. Voice-over that describes WITHOUT naming.

**Script template:**
> *"I've been working on something for [x] weeks.*
> *It's for the child who has never seen their language look beautiful.*
> *I'm not ready to show it yet.*
> *But I wanted you to know it exists.*
> *Follow. You'll see it first."*

**Visual:** Blurred pages. Ink swatches. Your hands but not the product. Close-up of a single illustrated detail — an eye from a character, one letter being drawn.

**Post this:** Every time you start a new book. Every time a major new product is 3–4 weeks from launch.

---

### FORMAT 3 — "The Decision" Vlog

**The mechanism:** You face a real creative decision and share it with your audience. Ask them to vote. Then honor their vote publicly.

**Examples:**

| The Decision | How You Share It | The Follow-Up |
|-------------|-----------------|---------------|
| *"Two covers for the new Mahabharata book. Which one?"* | Show both — face half-obscured, don't reveal which you prefer | Post the winning cover 3 days later: *"You chose. Here it is."* |
| *"Should the activity pages go before or after the story?"* | Brief VO explaining why it matters for a 4-year-old's attention | Announce the decision + why their input shaped it |
| *"Two illustrators. Two styles. One book. Help me choose."* | Side-by-side style comparison (no book title shown) | *"The style you picked is on pages 12–28. Now you're part of this book."* |

**Why it works:** The audience becomes **co-creators.** People buy things they helped build. This format also generates saves and DMs — both high-value signals.

---

### FORMAT 4 — "The Child Test" Series

**The mechanism:** You give a child (your own or someone else's) a book with NO explanation, NO direction. You film what happens. You don't narrate. You let the child's behavior sell the product.

**Shot structure:**
```
0:00–0:03  Book placed on table. Child approaches.
0:03–0:12  Child picks it up, opens it, begins exploring.
0:12–0:25  One genuine moment — they trace something, 
            laugh at an illustration, show someone else.
0:25–0:30  Your face watching. Small smile. 
           Text: "This is the whole reason."
```

**What you NEVER do in this format:**
- Point at the book and explain features
- Direct the child
- Show the cover clearly (let it be partially visible, not a showcase)
- Add a hard sales CTA

**Why it works:** You're not selling a book. You're showing **proof.** A parent watching a real child genuinely engaged with a product for 30 seconds trusts that more than any feature list.

Run this format once every 2–3 weeks with different children, different books, different ages.

---

### FORMAT 5 — "Day X of Building" Vlog Reel

**The mechanism:** Not a daily vlog — but **episodic.** Each reel is labeled *"Day 12 of building the new series"* or *"Week 3 of the Kannada collection."* Gaps between episodes are intentional — they create a sense that things are happening even when you're not posting.

**The episode structure (60–75 seconds):**

```
0:00–0:05  DAY [X] title card — simple, bold
0:05–0:20  One thing that's going well (brief, specific)
0:20–0:40  One thing that isn't (honest, no resolution yet)
0:40–0:55  One detail from the product — never the whole thing
0:55–0:70  One line to close — question or cliffhanger
```

**Closing line examples:**
- *"The illustrations came back today. They're not what I asked for. Day 13, we figure out why."*
- *"First child tested the book today. I don't want to say what happened yet. Day 16."*
- *"We're close. I can feel it. I also said that on Day 8. Day 22."*

**The number counts UP across all your content.** Even if you post Day 12 on a Tuesday and Day 15 on the following Saturday — the numbering implies that Days 13 and 14 **happened,** the work continued, the story moved forward without you narrating every moment. That gap builds more curiosity than filling it.

## THE OPEN LOOP TOOLKIT

These are specific techniques to embed in any reel to guarantee people come back:

| Technique | How to Use It |
|-----------|--------------|
| **The Unfinished Sentence** | End a VO mid-thought: *"And when I opened that sample, I — I'll show you what happened Thursday."* |
| **The Partial Reveal** | Show 30% of a book page. Blur the rest. *"Full reveal when we hit 5,000 followers."* Give them a reason to bring others. |
| **The Countdown** | *"6 days until the launch. I'm terrified."* Post on Day 6, Day 4, Day 2, Day 0. Each reel builds on the last. |
| **The Unexplained Reaction** | Film your genuine reaction to something — good or bad — without explaining what caused it. One line: *"I wasn't expecting that."* Let the comments ask. |
| **The "Ask Me"** | End any vlog reel with: *"One thing I can't show you yet — but DM me if you want to know what it is."* DMs are gold. Answer every one. |
| **The Callback** | Reference a previous reel explicitly: *"Three weeks ago I showed you a sketch. Today it's a real book. Here's what changed."* Rewards loyal viewers. |

## THE PRODUCT CURIOSITY LADDER

Think of your audience as climbing a ladder. Each rung requires a different kind of content:

```
RUNG 5 — BUYING
"I need this now."
Content: Child test + final reveal + social proof

RUNG 4 — WANTING  
"I can picture my child with this."
Content: Process documentary + decision reel

RUNG 3 — TRUSTING
"This person knows what she's doing."
Content: Education reels + behind-the-scenes struggle

RUNG 2 — INTERESTED
"I want to see what happens next."
Content: Unfinished book series + Day X vlog

RUNG 1 — AWARE
"Who is this?"
Content: Hook reel + origin story
```

**Most brands try to sell to people on Rung 1.** Your content strategy should move people up one rung per week — not push them to buy before they trust you.

The curiosity reels (Formats 1–5 above) are designed for **Rungs 2 and 3.** They are the bridge between awareness and desire. Most brands skip this entirely.

---

## PRACTICAL WEEKLY RHYTHM

Here's exactly what this looks like in practice — sustainable, not daily:

**Tuesday:** "Day X of Building" vlog episode — 60 sec, one open loop at the end

**Thursday:** Education reel OR child test video — trust builder, product adjacent

**Saturday:** Funny reel OR cultural story — reach and shares, no product pressure

**When a new book is 3 weeks from ready:** Activate the "Unfinished Book" series format — 6 episodes across 3 weeks, ending on launch day

**When you hit a milestone or setback:** Post a "Something Is Coming" or "Decision" reel immediately — real-time content always outperforms scheduled

---

## THE ONE RULE THAT GOVERNS ALL OF THIS

> **Never show the whole thing.**

Not the full book. Not the complete process. Not the entire story. Not the finished child reaction unedited.

Always leave one door slightly open. The viewer's imagination — what they *think* is behind that door — is more powerful than anything you could show them.

Your product is good enough that once people see it fully, they buy. The curiosity strategy exists to make sure they **care enough to look.**
