## Deethya Enterprises | March 31, 2026

# Executive Summary

SanskaTots operates in the Indian kids' educational books market on Amazon India — specifically across **three competitive segments**: (1) Activity/Velcro Busy Books, (2) Regional Language Tracing Books, and (3) Indian Mythology Story Books. This report profiles every significant competitor, evaluates what they do right and wrong, and identifies the **exact market gaps** SanskaTots can exploit.

**Key Finding:** No single competitor combines Indian cultural content + velcro interactivity + regional language depth. This intersection is SanskaTots' white space.

# PART 3: WHAT COMPETITORS DO WRONG (Exploit These Gaps)

## 1. The Cultural Void
**NOBODY** combines activity/interactive learning with Indian cultural content. Every velcro book seller (Clapjoy, Curious Cub, TodFod, Kidster, Hook n Loop, Intelliskills) sells generic Western-template content.

**Your Play:** Every SanskaTots velcro book should scream Indian identity — traditional outfits, Indian festivals, Indian food, Indian animals, Indian mythology.

## 2. The Regional Language Desert
Only InIkao and a few low-quality self-publishers serve the Kannada/Telugu/Tamil alphabet market. And they serve it BADLY — boring worksheets with no engagement design.

**Your Play:** Premium, beautifully illustrated regional language tracing books with cultural context. "ಅ for ಆನೆ (Elephant)" with gorgeous illustrations — not a drab workbook.

## 3. No Mythology + Activity Fusion
3,000+ mythology books for kids on Amazon. ZERO mythology activity books with velcro/interactive elements.

**Your Play:** "Ramayana Velcro Activity Book" — Match characters to scenes, dress Rama in different outfits across the story, sequence events with velcro pieces. This product DOES NOT EXIST on Amazon India.

## 4. No Age-Progressive Paths
Competitors sell individual books. Nobody sells a "learning path" — a clear Book 1 → Book 2 → Book 3 progression for a child's development.

**Your Play:** Create clear age bundles (2-year starter, 3-year explorer, 4-year scholar) and market the PROGRESSION, not just individual books.

## 5. No Community or Content Moat
Every competitor is a transactional Amazon seller. Nobody has a parenting community, educational content, or engaged social media.

**Your Play:** Instagram Reels of kids using books, parenting tips around Indian values, behind-the-scenes of book creation, "Why teach your child Kannada" content — this builds a moat no ad budget can buy.

## 6. No NRI-Focused Marketing
Indian diaspora parents are desperate for cultural learning tools for kids growing up abroad. No competitor explicitly targets them.

**Your Play:** Position SanskaTots as "the NRI parent's bridge to Indian culture." Amazon India ships globally. Your regional language books and mythology content are EXACTLY what NRI grandparents gift.
