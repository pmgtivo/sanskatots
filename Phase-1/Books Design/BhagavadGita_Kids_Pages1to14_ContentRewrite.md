## PAGE 1 — FOCUS ON ACTION, NOT RESULT (KARMA YOGA)

**Krishna Says:**
"You have the right to work, but never to the results of your work. Do your duty, and leave the rest to me."
*(Bhagavad Gita 2.47)*

**Explanation:**
Have you ever worked SO hard on a drawing for the school competition — and then didn't win? It feels awful, right? But here's Krishna's secret: the moment you stopped drawing and started thinking about the trophy, your drawing got WORSE! Karma Yoga means giving your 100% to the work and leaving the result to Krishna. The best exam you ever wrote was probably the one where you stopped worrying about marks and just FOCUSED on writing!

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A kid practises for a school singing competition — but keeps thinking: "I MUST win that trophy!" — nervously peeks at the judges
2. On stage — he gets so anxious about winning that he forgets the words — freezes, mouth open, no sound comes out
3. The next contestant sings with her eyes closed, smiling, enjoying every single note — not looking at the judges at all
4. She wins 2nd place but looks the happiest of everyone — "I just love singing!" — the frozen kid watches and finally understands

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna is backstage with a nervous kid just before he goes onstage. The kid is clutching a trophy picture instead of his song notes. Krishna gently takes the trophy picture away and replaces it with the song lyrics. He whispers: "Forget the trophy. Sing like you're singing just for ME — and that will be your best performance!"

## PAGE 2 — PRACTICE MAKES PERFECT (ABHYASA)

**Krishna Says:**
"The mind is restless and hard to control, but it can be trained through regular practice — one step at a time."
*(Bhagavad Gita 6.35)*

**Explanation:**
Why can Rohit Sharma hit a sixer so easily? Because he has played that same shot ten THOUSAND times in the nets! Nobody is born perfect — not great musicians, artists, doctors, or athletes. Every skill you have today — reading, writing, even tying your shoelaces — started because you practiced it again and again. The magic word in the Gita is ABHYASA — keep going, even when it feels hard and slow.

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A kid tries to draw a perfect circle — it comes out wobbly — "I'm TERRIBLE at drawing! I'll never be good!"
2. He slams his sketchbook shut, throws the pencil down, switches on the TV — "What's the point?"
3. His older sister opens HER sketchbook — first pages from a year ago have wobbly circles — latest pages have perfect ones — "I drew 50 circles every single day"
4. He starts a practice chart on his wall — one circle a day — one month later, his circles are perfect — he shows his class proudly

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna and a kid are sitting in a music room. The kid is learning tabla. On the wall behind them are tally marks: Day 1, Day 10, Day 50, Day 100. The tabla sound waves are shown getting smoother and stronger with each milestone. Krishna says: "Even I practised my flute every morning for years before it sang like the wind. Every master was once a beginner!"

## PAGE 3 — ANGER KILLS (KRODHA)

**Krishna Says:**
"From anger comes confusion. From confusion come wrong decisions. And wrong decisions destroy everything you have worked for."
*(Bhagavad Gita 2.62–63)*

**Explanation:**
Have you ever got so angry that you said something really mean to your best friend — and later wished with all your heart you could take it back? Anger is like a fire — it starts with a tiny spark but can burn everything around it. Krishna explains the exact chain: first you get angry, then your mind gets confused, then you do or say something you'll regret. The smartest thing to do when you feel anger rising? STOP. Take five slow, deep breaths before you speak or act.

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A kid loses a board game — he flips the board — pieces fly everywhere — "THIS GAME IS STUPID! I QUIT!"
2. One of the pieces hits his friend's eye — the friend stands up quietly and walks to the door — "I'm going home."
3. The kid sits alone in the messy room — pieces everywhere — the fun game ruined — "Why did I do that?"
4. He calls his friend — "I'm really sorry. I lost my temper. Can we start over?" — they rebuild the game together, both laughing

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna and a kid are standing next to a large volcano. The volcano is labelled "KRODHA — ANGER" and is starting to rumble. Krishna's hand is gently placed on the volcano's side, slowing it down. He looks at the kid and says: "Your anger is powerful energy. But used badly, it burns YOU first — before it burns anyone else!"

## PAGE 4 — BE STEADY-MINDED (SAMATVAM)

**Krishna Says:**
"Stay steady in success and failure. Treat both with the same calm mind. This steadiness of spirit is called Yoga."
*(Bhagavad Gita 2.48)*

**Explanation:**
Life is like a cricket pitch — sometimes the ball swings in your favour, and sometimes it doesn't! A great batsman doesn't jump wildly when he hits a six, OR throw his bat when he gets out for a duck. He STAYS STEADY. That's Samatvam — being cool-headed whether you win first prize or come last, whether your friend is kind to you or rude. Your happiness shouldn't depend on what the world does — it should come from inside you.

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A kid scores full marks in his exam — jumps around, brags to everyone — "I AM THE BEST!"
2. Next exam — he gets average marks — he sulks for three whole days, doesn't eat, cries, refuses to go to school
3. His grandmother holds both results in her hands — "One made you mean. One made you miserable. Is EITHER result really the problem?"
4. Third exam — he prepares calmly, gets good marks, smiles quietly and helps a classmate check her answers too — "I did my best. That's enough."

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna and a kid are sitting on opposite sides of a giant weighing scale. One side of the scale holds a shining first-place trophy. The other side holds a broken, cracked trophy. The scale is perfectly balanced. Krishna smiles and says: "A steady mind is stronger than any trophy — and cannot be taken away from you!"

## PAGE 5 — FRIEND OR ENEMY (ATMA-SHAKTI)

**Krishna Says:**
"You are your own best friend. And you can be your own worst enemy. It all depends on which voice inside you you choose to listen to."
*(Bhagavad Gita 6.5)*

**Explanation:**
The meanest bully you may ever meet is sometimes the voice INSIDE your own head! "I'm not smart enough." "I'll definitely fail." "Everyone else is better than me." That voice is your ENEMY. But there's also another voice — a kind, brave one — that says: "You've got this!" "Try one more time!" "You've done hard things before!" That voice is your BEST FRIEND. Every single day, you choose which voice to turn up and which one to ignore.

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A kid stares at a tough maths problem — inner bully whispers: "You're too dumb for this. Why even try? Just leave it."
2. He closes his book with a sigh — "I can't do it anyway" — lies on his bed doing nothing, feeling small
3. He sits up and remembers — "Wait. I solved even harder problems last month." — hears the kind voice: "Try once more. Just once."
4. He works through it step by step — solves it! — pumps his fist — "The biggest bully was inside my OWN head the whole time!"

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna stands between a kid and a tall mirror. In the mirror, a shadowy, grumpy version of the kid appears — arms crossed, frowning — this is the inner critic. Krishna points at the shadow and says: "This is the only enemy you truly need to defeat. And look — you are bigger, stronger, and brighter than it will ever be!"

## PAGE 6 — EAT LIKE A WINNER (SATTVIC DIET)

**Krishna Says:**
"Foods that give life, strength, happiness, and health — those are the foods of the pure and the wise."
*(Bhagavad Gita 17.8)*

**Explanation:**
What you eat every day literally BECOMES your body and brain! Fresh fruits, vegetables, milk, home-cooked dal-rice, nuts, and curd — these make your brain sharp, your body strong, and your mind calm. That's the SATTVIC way. But too much junk food — oily chips, sugary cola, packaged snacks — makes you tired, cloudy-headed, and cranky. Even a Formula 1 racing car needs the RIGHT fuel. Would you pour dirty water into a champion's engine? Then why put junk into yours?

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A kid eats only chips, cola, and chocolates at a birthday party — feels amazing in the moment — "MORE PLEASE!"
2. That night — stomach pain, can't sleep, too hyper, tossing and turning — "Why do I feel SO bad?!"
3. Next morning at school — can't concentrate, yawning, grumpy, head heavy — misses every question in the class quiz
4. He sees his friend eating an apple and a glass of milk at snack time — she wins the quiz, alert and smiling — he switches snacks the very next day

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna is in a bright, colourful kitchen with a modern kid. The table is split in two: one half has chips, cola, and candy with wilted flowers and a sad face emoji; the other half has fruits, dal, rice, milk, and a salad with blooming flowers and a happy face. Krishna holds up a bright orange and says: "This tiny orange has more energy than ten packets of chips. YOUR body knows the difference — even if your tongue doesn't yet!"

## PAGE 7 — MASTER YOUR SENSES (INDRIYA NIGRAHA)

**Krishna Says:**
"Just as a strong wind sweeps a boat far off course, even one runaway sense can carry your entire mind far away from your goal."
*(Bhagavad Gita 2.67)*

**Explanation:**
Imagine you sit down to study for tomorrow's big test. Suddenly — you hear the TV in the next room. Then you smell Pizza. Your phone buzzes. Now your ears, your nose, and your eyes have ALL pulled your mind away from your books! Your senses are powerful — but YOU must be their captain, not their slave. Indriya Nigraha means deciding WHEN to eat, WHEN to watch TV, WHEN to use your phone — instead of letting those things decide for you.

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A kid sits at his study table — phone buzzes — "Just ONE quick check, it'll take 5 seconds..."
2. He's now lying on the bed watching videos — eating chips — it's dark outside — "Wait... what happened to studying?"
3. Clock shows 11 PM — exam is at 8 AM tomorrow — textbook still closed — "How did I WASTE the whole evening?!"
4. Next evening — he puts his phone in a drawer with a sticky note: "OPEN AFTER STUDY" — studies one focused hour — done by 6 PM — free time AND good preparation!

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna and a kid are together at a ship's steering wheel, navigating through a stormy ocean. The waves crashing around them are labelled: "Phone," "TV," "Snacks," "Notifications," "Distractions." The kid grips the wheel tightly. Krishna stands beside him with a calm, steady hand and says: "YOU are the captain of your mind. Don't let the waves choose your direction — YOU do!"

## PAGE 8 — DO YOUR OWN DUTY (SWADHARMA)

**Krishna Says:**
"It is far better to do your own duty — even imperfectly — than to perfectly copy someone else's path."
*(Bhagavad Gita 3.35)*

**Explanation:**
Have you ever given up something YOU love — painting, dancing, cooking — because your friends said cricket or coding was cooler? The Gita says: STOP! Your path is YOURS alone. A rose that tries to be a lotus will fail. But a rose that blooms as a rose? MAGNIFICENT. Your special gift — whatever it is — is what the world is waiting for. It doesn't matter if it's different from everyone else's. Actually, BECAUSE it's different, it matters more!

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A kid who LOVES painting is pressured to join the cricket team because all his friends play — "Art is for girls! Come play cricket with us!"
2. He drags himself to cricket every day — plays terribly, sits on the bench, misses his art class — stares at his paints sadly from the window
3. He visits a school art exhibition — meets a young artist showing his work — "I was also told art was 'not serious.' I kept going anyway."
4. He rejoins art club — creates a stunning painting — wins the state-level art competition — friends come to congratulate him: "WE should have tried this!"

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna and a kid stand in front of a large wall covered in different paths — one path has a cricket bat, one has a paintbrush, one has a microscope, one has a musical note. Each path glows with its own colour. Krishna says: "The world doesn't need another copy. It's already got one of those. It NEEDS the original, irreplaceable you!"

## PAGE 9 — FEARLESSNESS (ABHAYAM)

**Krishna Says:**
"Fearlessness, purity of heart, and wisdom — these are divine qualities, and they are already living inside you."
*(Bhagavad Gita 16.1)*

**Explanation:**
Every person on earth is scared of something — the dark, exams, speaking in class, making new friends, trying something new. Being brave does NOT mean having no fear. It means feeling the fear AND doing it anyway! Even the great warrior Arjuna was trembling on the battlefield before Krishna spoke to him. But he CHOSE to stand up. That same courage Krishna gave Arjuna? It's already sleeping inside YOU — waiting for you to wake it up.

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A kid is called to give a speech at Morning Assembly — heart pounding, palms sweating — "I can't do it! I'll forget everything! Everyone will laugh!"
2. He hides behind the stage curtain — teacher calls his name — he's frozen to the spot, unable to move
3. His friend peeks behind the curtain — "Remember last year when you were scared to climb that tree? You climbed it! And then you climbed it AGAIN just for fun!"
4. He walks onstage — voice trembles at first — then grows steady and clear — the whole school claps — "Being brave is something you DO. Not something you wait to FEEL."

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna stands backstage with a kid who is about to walk into bright stage lights. The kid's knees are shaking and his school speech notes are crumpled in his fist. Krishna places one hand on the kid's back, gently nudging him forward. He says: "The lion inside you is waiting to roar. All you have to do is take ONE step toward the light — and the lion will take care of the rest!"

## PAGE 10 — SEE EVERYONE AS EQUAL (SAMA-DARSHANA)

**Krishna Says:**
"The truly wise see the same soul shining in a learned scholar, a cow, an elephant, a poor man, and an outcast."
*(Bhagavad Gita 5.18)*

**Explanation:**
Some kids at your school might wear better shoes. Some might have less. Some might speak different languages, eat different food, or pray in different ways. But inside EVERY single person — rich or poor, tall or short, any religion, any background — is the same precious soul, the same inner light. Krishna says the wisest, most beautiful people are those who see THAT light in everyone around them — not just the people who look and think exactly like them.

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A new kid joins the school — dresses differently, speaks with a different accent — sits alone in the canteen — nobody invites him over
2. A group of kids look at him and whisper: "He's different. Don't sit with him." — they turn their backs
3. The new kid quietly helps another student pick up all the books she dropped — big, genuine smile — "Here, let me help!"
4. One kid who watched sees the smile — walks over — "Hi, I'm Arjun. Want to sit with us?" — "I'm Rajan. Thank you!" — everyone laughs together at lunch

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna sits in the centre of a big circle of children — kids from different states, wearing different clothes, carrying different lunchboxes. They are all sharing food from each other's boxes. Krishna has his arms open wide, and says: "When you look at someone and see the same light that lives in YOU — that is the wisest thing a human being can do!"

## PAGE 11 — GOOD COMPANY MATTERS (SATSANG)

**Krishna Says:**
"Those who always share stories of love and goodness — they live in joy and fill the world around them with light."
*(Bhagavad Gita 10.9)*

**Explanation:**
Have you noticed that you feel HAPPY and energetic around some friends, but tired and drained around others? That's because people's energy rubs off on you — like colours mixing in a painting! If you spend time with friends who always cheat, gossip, bully, or waste time — slowly, slowly, you start doing the same, without even realising it. But if your friends are curious, kind, and hardworking — you become MORE of those things too. Choose your company like you choose your food — pick the things that make you healthier!

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A hard-working kid starts hanging out with a lazy group — "Skip homework! Nobody checks anyway!" — he laughs along and skips it too
2. One week becomes one month — marks drop, teacher calls parents, he stops trying — "I used to love school…"
3. An old classmate finds him outside — "Come see our robotics club! We're building a solar-powered car!" — eyes light up
4. He joins the club — works with enthusiastic, curious kids — wins the inter-school science fair — "The people around you really do become the person you become!"

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna and a kid sit around a campfire with three friends. Words are floating up with the sparks: "Kindness," "Curiosity," "Courage," "Laughter," "Honesty." The fire is warm and bright. Krishna looks at the kid and says: "Choose friends who add fuel to your fire — not ones who quietly pour water on it when you're not looking!"

## PAGE 12 — GIVING WITHOUT EXPECTATION (SEVA)

**Krishna Says:**
"Perform your actions as an offering — without keeping score, without expecting reward. That selfless work is called true Seva."
*(Bhagavad Gita 3.9)*

**Explanation:**
Have you ever helped someone and then secretly waited for them to say "Thank you" — and felt annoyed when they didn't? That waiting and counting is NOT seva! True seva is giving simply because it FEELS RIGHT in your heart — like watering a plant without demanding it grow faster for you. When you help someone purely because you want to — without any scorekeeping — something wonderful happens: YOU end up feeling happier than the person you helped!

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A kid helps an elderly neighbour carry her heavy grocery bags all the way to her door — she smiles and thanks him warmly
2. He waits by the lift — she goes inside — door closes — "She didn't give me anything... not even a toffee!"
3. He walks home grumbling. But that night — he sleeps deeply, feels warm and peaceful — dreams are sweet — wakes up smiling
4. The next week he helps carry someone else's bags — doesn't wait for thanks — just walks away with a quiet smile — "THIS feeling. This is what giving really is."

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna and a kid are volunteering in a school library — sorting books, helping younger students reach the top shelves, reading aloud to a small group. The kid is completely absorbed and happy. A soft golden light glows around him — he doesn't notice it, but Krishna does. Krishna steps back, smiling, and says: "When you give without keeping score — the whole universe begins keeping score FOR you. And its memory is perfect!"

## PAGE 13 — EVERYTHING CHANGES (IMPERMANENCE)

**Krishna Says:**
"The sensations of heat and cold, happiness and sadness — they come and go, like seasons. They never stay forever. Be patient through them."
*(Bhagavad Gita 2.14)*

**Explanation:**
Have you ever had a truly TERRIBLE day at school — lost your lunch box, fought with your best friend, failed a surprise test — and thought "My WHOLE life is ruined"? But what happened the next week? You probably can't even fully remember the details! That's exactly what Krishna means — nothing lasts forever. Not sadness, not pain, not anger, not even happiness! The monsoon rains pour hard every year — but so does the summer sunshine come back. Even the darkest, longest night has a sunrise quietly waiting on the other side.

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A kid's best friend's family is moving to another city — "I will NEVER be happy again. My life is over. I've lost my only real friend!"
2. He cries for days — won't eat, won't play — locks his bedroom door — stares at the ceiling
3. His grandfather shows him a box of old letters — "My best friend moved to Pune when I was your age. We wrote letters every week." — the letters are tied in a bundle, yellowed with age and use
4. He video calls his friend that same evening — they talk and laugh for an hour — "Distance changes things. But friendship? Only if you let it."

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna and a kid are sitting on a hillside together, watching a sky that shows all four seasons at once — one quadrant has monsoon clouds and rain, one has bright summer sun, one has autumn falling leaves, one has spring blossoms. Krishna points at the whole sky and says: "Every season of your life has a gift hidden inside it. Especially the rainy ones — those are the ones that make everything grow!"

## PAGE 14 — YOU ARE NEVER ALONE

**Krishna Says:**
"I live in the heart of every being. Surrender your fears to me, and I will carry you through every storm that comes."
*(Bhagavad Gita 18.61 & 18.66)*

**Explanation:**
Do you ever feel lonely — like nobody really understands you? Maybe it's late at night and everyone's asleep, but you're lying awake worrying? Here is Krishna's most beautiful promise in the WHOLE Gita: he is ALWAYS with you — not only in the temple, but inside your very own heart, right now, as you read this. Every time you feel scared or alone, put one hand on your heart, close your eyes, take a slow deep breath, and know: the one who created this entire universe is sitting right HERE, listening, and he is never going anywhere.

**4-Panel Comic Strip:**
1. A kid is sick at home — parents are at work, grandma is resting, house is very quiet — "Nobody is here. Nobody cares. I'm all alone."
2. He feels small and scared — tears roll down his cheek — ceiling fan turns slowly — he stares at nothing
3. He turns and sees the small picture of Baby Krishna on his shelf — Krishna's painted eyes seem to look directly at him, warmly — he picks it up
4. He whispers quietly: "Krishna… I'm scared." — a slow, gentle calm spreads through his chest — he smiles a tiny smile — "It's okay. I'm not alone. I never was."

**Main Illustration:**
Baby Krishna is sitting right beside a kid in a dimly lit bedroom, one gentle arm around the kid's shoulder. The kid cannot see Krishna — but Krishna's form glows softly blue-gold, filling the room with warmth and light. The kid has a small, peaceful smile on his face. Krishna looks at the reader and says: "Even when you cannot see me, I am here. I was here before you were born. I will be here always. You are NEVER alone."
