## DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

> **Age 2–3 is the "explosion year."** Vocabulary triples. The child begins to categorize the world — animals, colors, shapes, people, feelings. Every page in this book must mirror the Indian world the child actually lives in — not a Western textbook world of blonde children and igloos.

### The 5 Design Rules for Every Page
1. **One activity, one page** — no cramming. White space is learning space.
2. **Indian visual world** — if counting, count diyas. If matching, match rangoli patterns. If coloring, color a peacock.
3. **Bilingual label on every illustration** — every object labeled in English + Kannada
4. **Large print, thick outlines** — minimum 3pt stroke on all coloring outlines (chubby crayons need thick lines)
5. **Activities go from EASY (left side of book) to HARDER (right side)** — built-in progression keeps the child in "flow"

## SECTION A — PRE-WRITING & MOTOR SKILLS (Pages 1–8)

*Purpose: Build pencil control before letters and numbers. Every tracing path has an Indian theme so the child is learning culture while building motor skills.*

---

### Page 1 — Trace the River: Wavy Lines
**Activity:** Trace the wavy lines (3 lines, increasing difficulty: gentle wave → medium wave → rapid wave)  
**Indian Scene:** A river (Ganga/Cauvery) flowing from mountains to the sea — a boat is riding each wave line  
**Instruction:** "Help the boat sail down the river! Trace the lines."  
**Bilingual:** "River / ನದಿ", "Boat / ದೋಣಿ"  
**Motor skill:** Wrist flexibility, continuous stroke

---

### Page 2 — Light the Diyas: Vertical Lines
**Activity:** Trace vertical lines (top to bottom) — 5 lines of increasing length  
**Indian Scene:** 5 diyas in a row on a rangoli mat. Each diya has a dotted wick that needs to be "lit" with a vertical stroke  
**Instruction:** "Light each diya! Trace straight down to light the flame."  
**Bilingual:** "Diya / ದೀಪ", "Flame / ಜ್ವಾಲೆ"  
**Motor skill:** Top-to-bottom stroke (foundation for writing letters like l, i, t)

---

### Page 3 — Draw the Banana Leaf: Horizontal Lines
**Activity:** Trace horizontal lines (left to right) — 5 lines on a banana leaf  
**Indian Scene:** A large banana leaf outline with dotted horizontal lines across it (like the veins of the leaf)  
**Instruction:** "Trace the lines on the banana leaf — left to right!"  
**Bilingual:** "Banana Leaf / ಬಾಳೆ ಎಲೆ"  
**Motor skill:** Left-to-right directionality (pre-reading direction)

---

### Page 4 — Fly the Kite: Diagonal Lines
**Activity:** Trace diagonal lines (/ and \) — kite strings going up and down  
**Indian Scene:** 6 kites in the sky (Makar Sankranti / Uttarayan setting), each kite connected by a diagonal dotted string to a child's hand below  
**Instruction:** "Trace the kite strings! Don't let go!"  
**Bilingual:** "Kite / ಗಾಳಿಪಟ", "Sky / ಆಕಾಶ"  
**Motor skill:** Diagonal strokes (foundation for A, V, W, X, K, Z, M, N)

---

### Page 5 — Draw the Rangoli: Curved Lines
**Activity:** Trace curved paths forming a simple rangoli petal pattern (open curves)  
**Indian Scene:** A half-drawn rangoli — dotted curves that the child completes to form the full flower  
**Instruction:** "Finish the rangoli! Trace the dotted curves."  
**Bilingual:** "Rangoli / ರಂಗೋಲಿ"  
**Motor skill:** Curved stroke, smooth hand movement

---

### Page 6 — Roll the Laddoo: Circles
**Activity:** Trace circles — 5 circles of increasing size (large to small)  
**Indian Scene:** A grandmother's kitchen — each circle is a laddoo being rolled in her hands, with dotted circle outline  
**Instruction:** "Help Paati roll the laddoos! Trace each circle."  
**Bilingual:** "Laddoo / ಲಾಡು", "Grandmother / ಅಜ್ಜಿ"  
**Motor skill:** Closed circular stroke (foundation for letters o, c, d, g, q, e)

---

### Page 7 — Trace the Peacock Feather: Complex Curves
**Activity:** Trace a peacock feather outline — an open curve with an eye at the top (teardrops)  
**Indian Scene:** A large, beautiful peacock (India's national bird) with dotted feather outlines to trace  
**Instruction:** "Trace the peacock's beautiful feathers!"  
**Bilingual:** "Peacock / ನವಿಲು", "Feather / ಗರಿ"  
**Motor skill:** Combined curves (more complex, bridging to letter formation)

---

### Page 8 — Maze: Help the Cow Find Her Calf 🐄
**Activity:** Simple maze (wide paths, 2 wrong turns) — child draws a path through the maze  
**Indian Scene:** A decorated Indian cow (with flower garland) at the start; her calf waiting in a stable at the end  
**Instruction:** "Help the cow find her baby! Draw the path."  
**Bilingual:** "Cow / ಹಸು", "Calf / ಕರು"  
**Motor skill:** Directional control, planning, pencil lifting practice
