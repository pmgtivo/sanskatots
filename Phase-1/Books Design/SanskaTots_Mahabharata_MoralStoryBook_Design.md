## DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

### Core Principle: Every Page Is a Mini Comic
Each of the 22 story pages works like a **4–6 panel comic strip**. Sequential panels tell the story like a cartoon strip — exactly how toddlers and early readers absorb stories best.

### Target Audience
- **Primary (Kids 2–5):** Cannot read. Panels guide the eye left → right, top → bottom. Big expressive faces, clear actions, vivid Indian colors.
- **Secondary (Parents):** Want a clear moral they can discuss. The **Modern Life Moral** on each page connects the ancient story to the child's world today.

### Design Rules

| Rule | Implementation |
|---|---|
| **80% Illustration / 20% Text** | Each panel has max 1 short caption (5–8 words). Title + Moral box below all panels. |
| **Comic Panel Grid** | 4 panels = 2×2 grid. 5 panels = 2+3 or 3+2 layout. 6 panels = 3×2 grid. |
| **Big Faces, Big Emotions** | All characters in **chibi/kawaii** style — oversized heads, huge eyes, exaggerated expressions |
| **No Scary or Dark Imagery** | Duryodhana = grumpy/jealous not evil. Battles = cartoon action. No blood, no fear. |
| **Warm Indian Palette** | Deep saffron, royal blue, forest green, golden yellow, peacock teal — bright and joyful |
| **Consistent Characters** | Same chibi style for all characters across all 22 pages |
| **Modern Life Moral** | Every story ends with how the lesson connects to a child's **real daily life today** |
| **Speech Bubbles** | Key character lines shown in round speech bubbles inside panels |
| **Panel Numbering** | Subtle ① ② ③ numbers in corner of each panel to guide reading order |

### PAGE 1 — "Look at ONLY the Bird's Eye!" 🎯
**Theme: Focus and Concentration | Modern Moral: When you do a task, focus completely — do ONE thing at a time**

**Panel Grid: 5 panels (2 top + 3 bottom)**

| Panel | Caption Text | Illustration Brief |
|---|---|---|
| **① Panel 1** | *Guru Drona called all his students.* | Forest gurukul scene. All chibi students gathered under a big tree — Arjuna, Duryodhana, Bhima, others. Drona (tall, dignified, kind face, long beard, teacher's staff) standing before them. A toy bird sitting in the tree. |
| **② Panel 2** | *He pointed to a bird in the tree.* | Drona pointing up at the tree. Students shading their eyes, all looking up. The bird perched on a branch. Drona's expression: curious, testing. Speech bubble: "Aim at the bird. Tell me what you see." |
| **③ Panel 3** | *"I see the tree, sky, leaves, bird…"* | One by one other students answer — speech bubbles showing: "I see the tree!" "I see the sky!" "I see many leaves!" Their expressions: confident but unfocused. Drona nodding slowly but not smiling. |
| **④ Panel 4** | *Arjuna said, "I see ONLY the bird's eye."* | Close-up of Arjuna — one eye closed, bow raised, arrow aimed. His face: completely still, deeply focused. ONLY the bird's eye is shown in sharp focus; everything else is blurred around him. Speech bubble: "Only the eye, Guruji." |
| **⑤ Panel 5** | *"SHOOT!" Arjuna hit it perfectly!* | Action panel! Arrow flying with a golden trail — straight to the bird's eye (the bird's eye pops off like a cartoon bullseye). Drona's face: beaming with pride, hands raised. All other students: jaws dropped in amazement. |

**🪷 MODERN LIFE MORAL:** When you do your homework or practice — put your phone away and focus on ONE thing. That is how champions are made!

## PANEL ILLUSTRATION PRODUCTION NOTES

### For the Illustrator

1. **Consistency First:** Create a **master character sheet** before drawing a single panel. All 10 core characters must look identical across all 22 pages.
2. **Panel Emotions:** Each panel tells ONE emotion clearly. Don't crowd panels with multiple emotions.
3. **Reading Flow:** Panels must be numbered ① ② ③ visually for left to right, top to bottom reading.
4. **Speech Bubbles:** Round bubbles for speech. Cloud-shape for thoughts. Keep max 6 words per bubble.
5. **Action Lines:** Use comic-style speed lines for dynamic moments.
6. **Expressions Library:** For each character create an expression reference sheet — Happy, Sad, Angry, Scared, Brave, Proud, Surprised, Focused.
7. **Background Complexity:** Simple and clear — one strong background element per panel (forest, palace, river, chariot). Never busy.
8. **Moral Box Design:** Every page — same lotus-border box at bottom. Peacock-teal background, white bold text. A consistent element kids can always find.
9. **Mahabharata Visual Identity:** Include subtle chariot wheel (Dharma Chakra) and peacock motifs as decorative elements — different from the Ramayana lotus/temple arch style.
10. **Colour Theme Distinction:** This book uses PEACOCK TEAL as its primary border color (vs saffron orange in the Ramayana book) — making the two books visually distinct on a shelf.

### Software and Technical Specs
- **Format:** Adobe Illustrator (vector) preferred for print quality
- **Resolution:** 300 DPI minimum, CMYK color mode
